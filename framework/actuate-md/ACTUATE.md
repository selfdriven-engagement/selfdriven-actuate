# ACTUATE — selfdriven Actuation Framework Reference

Session context for building materials, documents, sessions, and systems grounded in the selfdriven Actuation frameworks published at [actuate.selfdriven.community](https://actuate.selfdriven.community).

---

## Overview

The selfdriven Actuation platform is a collection of interlocking frameworks for **community and organisational self-actuation** — the process by which people, teams, and communities move from awareness to capability to sustained contribution. The frameworks are open, composable, and designed to be applied together rather than in isolation.

### Core Principle

> Communities and organisations do not need to be managed into effectiveness. They need the conditions — skills, structure, rhythm, and trust — to actuate themselves.

Actuation is not one-time. It is a **progressive, iterative cycle** of understanding context → identifying capability → building capacity → delivering outcomes → reflecting and improving.

## 1. Progressive Self-Actuation Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework)

### What It Is

A structured analytical approach combining the **skillzeb.io universal skill set** with the **5W1H method** to help communities understand their own context deeply enough to drive effective, targeted development.

### The 5W1H Lens

Apply these six questions in order when scoping any actuation initiative, programme, or community intervention:

| # | Question | Actuation Focus |
|---|----------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Why** | What is the rationale? How will this capability benefit the community and its overarching objectives? |
| 2 | **What** | Which specific skills, behaviours, or structures are needed? Map to skillzeb.io Skill Set Explorer. |
| 3 | **Who** | Who already has these skills? Who needs them? Identify existing talent and development targets. |
| 4 | **When** | What are the timelines? When do initiatives begin? What milestones mark progress? |
| 5 | **Where** | Where are these skills most needed? Which projects, teams, or geographic areas? |
| 6 | **How** | What are the acquisition and integration strategies? Use skillzeb.io Template Explorer for scaffolding. |

### Benefits of This Approach

- **Comprehensive Skill Mapping** — assesses the full landscape, not just the visible gaps
- **Targeted Development** — focuses resources on specific needs, maximising impact
- **Enhanced Collaboration** — shared clarity on who holds what and who needs what
- **Strategic Planning** — produces structured action plans with defined roles, timelines, and objectives

## 2. Engagement–Lab–Production (ELP) Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/engagement-lab-production](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/engagement-lab-production)

### What It Is

A three-phase progression model for **continuous actuation** — moving people, ideas, and work through increasing levels of commitment and rigour before they enter production.

### The Three Phases

```
ENGAGEMENT  →  LAB  →  PRODUCTION
  (explore)    (test)   (deliver)
```

| Phase | Purpose | Characteristics |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| **Engagement** | Attract, orient, and onboard | Low commitment, high curiosity, safe to explore and ask questions |
| **Lab** | Experiment, prototype, validate | Medium commitment, iterative, failure is expected and useful |
| **Production** | Execute, maintain, improve | Full commitment, quality standards, accountability for outcomes |

### When to Apply

- Designing onboarding journeys for new community members
- Deciding where a project or workstream belongs in its lifecycle
- Structuring capability-building programmes that graduate participants
- Preventing premature production commitment (i.e., putting Engagement-phase ideas into Production prematurely)

## 3. Areas of Focus (8 AoF) Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/areas-of-focus](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/areas-of-focus)

### What It Is

An eight-domain organisational capability map used to **structure roles, responsibilities, and resource allocation** across a self-actuating community or organisation. Each Area of Focus represents a critical function that must be healthy for the whole system to thrive.

### The Eight Areas

| # | Area of Focus | Core Function |
|---|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | **Identity & Trust** | Who we are, how we are known, and how trust is established (cryptographic and social) |
| 2 | **Capability & Skills** | What we can do — individual and collective competency |
| 3 | **Community & Culture** | How we relate, collaborate, and sustain shared norms |
| 4 | **Governance & Compliance** | How decisions are made, rules upheld, and accountability maintained |
| 5 | **Resources & Finance** | How energy (money, time, compute) flows in and out |
| 6 | **Technology & Infrastructure** | The platforms, tools, and systems that support operations |
| 7 | **Services & Delivery** | What we produce and deliver to members and the broader world |
| 8 | **Learning & Intelligence** | How we grow, adapt, and improve over time |

### When to Apply

- Designing team or organisational structures
- Conducting a community health audit
- Allocating responsibilities across contributors
- Identifying gaps in community capability
- Structuring governance documents or operational frameworks

### Usage in AI Context

When a Human Conductor model is in use, map each AI agent or AI-augmented role to one or more Areas of Focus. Agents should not span all eight — they should have a primary AoF and be explicitly handed off when crossing into others.

## 4. Collaborative Day Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/the-collaborative-day](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/the-collaborative-day)

### What It Is

A rhythmic daily structure that organises an organisation or community's working day into intentional zones of activity, preventing the loss of focused work time to unstructured interruption.

### Core Idea

The day is not uniform. Different hours are optimised for different kinds of attention. Structure the day to honour this, rather than fighting it.

### Relationship to 5Cs

The Collaborative Day Framework provides the **macro-structure** (day-level). The 5Cs Collaborative Session (below) provides the **micro-structure** (session-level). They nest: a Collaborative Day contains one or more Collaborative Sessions, each of which runs through the 5Cs arc.

## 5. The 5Cs Collaborative Session Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/the-collaborative-5cs](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/the-collaborative-5cs)

### What It Is

A session-level framework that structures any collaborative gathering — standup, workshop, sprint, or full day — around **five intentional zones of attention**. Rather than expecting participants to operate in the same mental mode throughout, the session moves deliberately through five named modes.

### The Five Cs

```
C1 Check-In → C2 Curious → C3 Caring → C4 Constructive → C5 Check-Out
 (Arrive)      (Explore)    (Align)       (Build)           (Depart)
```

#### C1 — Check-In: Arrive & Orient

**Purpose:** Create a shared moment of arrival that transitions participants from individual mode into collaborative mode. Everyone starts the session seen, heard, and oriented.

**Typical Activities:**
- Personal arrival — marking presence for the session
- Sharing current state: energy, focus, intentions
- Surfacing blockers or carry-overs from the previous session
- Setting personal and shared intentions
- Quick pulse on wellbeing and capacity

**Characteristics:** Grounding, brief, structured, inclusive. No problem-solving — just orientation. Psychological safety is paramount.

---

#### C2 — Curious: Explore & Learn

**Purpose:** Expand the group's understanding and surface opportunities before committing to action.

**Typical Activities:**
- Research sharing
- Asking open questions
- Reviewing new ideas and emerging concepts
- Early-stage brainstorming
- Exploring new technologies or patterns

**Characteristics:** Open conversation, low pressure, intellectual exploration. Unfinished ideas are welcome.

---

#### C3 — Caring: Align & Connect

**Purpose:** Ensure people are aligned and supported before entering deeper execution work.

**Typical Activities:**
- Mentoring and guidance
- Coordination discussions
- Resolving blockers
- Supporting community members experiencing difficulty
- Celebrating progress and contributions

**Characteristics:** Empathy, listening, shared awareness, trust-building. Psychological safety continues from C1.

---

#### C4 — Constructive: Build & Deliver

**Purpose:** Transform curiosity and alignment into tangible results.

**Typical Activities:**
- Development, creation, crafting
- Project execution and delivery
- Writing and documentation
- Design and production
- Testing, verification, and reflection on work product

**Characteristics:** Focused attention, minimal interruptions, progress toward outcomes, accountability for delivery.

---

#### C5 — Check-Out: Reflect & Close

**Purpose:** Create a shared moment of closure that honours the session's work, captures learning, and allows participants to consciously depart. No one leaves mid-sentence.

**Typical Activities:**
- Sharing progress and lessons learned
- Acknowledging contributions
- Flagging items for the next session
- Reflecting on what worked and what didn't
- Intentional closing — marking the end of the collaborative session

**Characteristics:** Gratitude, recognition, honest reflection, clean handover to the next session. Transition out of collaborative mode.

---

### Example Day Session Schedule

| Time | Zone | Focus |
|------|------|-------|
| 08:45 – 09:00 | C1 Check-In | Arrival, presence, intentions, capacity pulse |
| 09:00 – 10:00 | C2 Curious | Learning, ideas, research sharing, exploration |
| 10:00 – 11:00 | C3 Caring | Mentoring, alignment, coordination, blocker resolution |
| 11:00 – 15:00 | C4 Constructive | Deep work, project delivery, building, and creation |
| 15:00 – 15:30 | C5 Check-Out | Progress sharing, reflection, acknowledgement, clean close |

*Timing is adaptable — the sequence matters more than the clock.*

---

### 5Cs Design Principles

When designing or facilitating a 5Cs session:

1. **Honour the arc** — do not skip C1 or C5. The temptation to jump straight to C4 is what destroys collaborative culture over time.
2. **Name the zone** — explicitly announce which zone the session is entering. Synchronisation of expectations is the key value.
3. **Protect C4** — the constructive zone is the most vulnerable to interruption. Treat it as sacred.
4. **C3 before C4** — never move to construction if alignment and blockers have not been addressed.
5. **C5 is not optional** — closure is a collective act. Drifting away mid-flow leaves the session and the people in it unfinished.

## 6. Input–Injected Intelligence–Output (IIO) Framework

**Source:** [selfdriven.ai/input-injected-intelligence-output-framework](https://selfdriven.ai/input-injected-intelligence-output-framework)

### What It Is

A model for understanding how AI augments human work within a self-actuating community. Rather than replacing human decision-making, AI **injects intelligence** into the flow between input and output.

### The Three Elements

```
INPUT  →  [INJECTED INTELLIGENCE]  →  OUTPUT
(Human context, intent, data)    (AI reasoning, pattern, generation)    (Enhanced human decision or artefact)
```

### Principles

- The human provides **context and intent** (the Input)
- AI provides **reasoning, synthesis, or generation** (the Injected Intelligence)
- The human **accepts, modifies, or rejects** the AI contribution before it becomes Output
- The loop repeats — Output becomes Input for the next cycle

### Application

Use IIO framing when:
- Designing AI-assisted workflows within a selfdriven property
- Explaining AI's role to community members unfamiliar with AI-native working
- Structuring prompts or agent pipelines where the human-in-the-loop point must be explicit

## 7. AI-Assisted Actuation Framework

**Source:** [actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/ai-assisted-actuation](https://actuate.selfdriven.community/framework/ai-assisted-actuation)

### What It Is

Guidance for integrating AI tools and agents into community actuation work in a way that enhances rather than supplants human capability and judgment.

### Core Principles

1. **AI amplifies actuation — it does not replace it.** The human conductor remains responsible for direction, values, and outcomes.
2. **Transparency first.** Community members should understand when AI is contributing to outputs they receive or act upon.
3. **Match AI to AoF.** Deploy AI assistance in the Areas of Focus where it has the clearest value (e.g., Learning & Intelligence, Technology & Infrastructure) before more sensitive areas (e.g., Community & Culture, Governance).
4. **IIO loop is non-negotiable.** No AI output enters production without a human review and acceptance step.
5. **Capability building, not dependency building.** The goal of AI assistance is to raise the community's own capability — not create reliance on a tool or vendor.

## Vocabulary Reference

Use these terms consistently in all selfdriven actuation materials:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Actuation** | The process of a community or organisation moving from potential to active, self-directed contribution |
| **Self-Actuating** | Capable of sustaining its own growth, governance, and delivery without external management |
| **Human Conductor** | The human role that directs AI agents and maintains accountability for outputs |
| **Collaborative Session** | A structured gathering using the 5Cs framework |
| **Zone** | One of the five named modes within a 5Cs session (C1–C5) |
| **Area of Focus (AoF)** | One of the eight organisational capability domains |
| **ELP Phase** | The current stage of a project or person in the Engagement–Lab–Production lifecycle |
| **Injected Intelligence** | AI contribution inserted between human input and human-reviewed output |
| **Skill** | A discrete, mappable capability from the skillzeb.io universal skill set |
| **Community Design** | The intentional structuring of a community's context, roles, rhythms, and relationships |

## Quick Application Guide

### "I need to design a workshop / session"
→ Apply the **5Cs framework**. Map activities to C1–C5 zones. Protect C4. Do not skip C1 or C5.

### "I need to understand what a community needs"
→ Apply the **Progressive Self-Actuation Framework**. Walk through Why → What → Who → When → Where → How.

### "I need to structure an organisation or team"
→ Apply the **8 Areas of Focus**. Map every role and responsibility to an AoF. Identify gaps.

### "I need to onboard new contributors or graduate projects"
→ Apply the **ELP Framework**. Place people and projects in the right phase. Define graduation criteria.

### "I need to integrate AI into community work"
→ Apply the **IIO Framework** and **AI-Assisted Actuation** principles. Establish the human review loop. Map AI roles to AoFs.

### "I'm writing a collaborative day plan"
→ Use the **Collaborative Day Framework** as the macro structure. Embed one or more **5Cs sessions** within it.
